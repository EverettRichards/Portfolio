4/1/26

The air hangs thick with sticky steam
like houses soiled down,
whose doldrums weep and ceilings creak
when tempest strikes the town.

Decatur Street is wide awake
as it snakes along the levee,
which beckons gold and malt and wheat
from barges full and heavy.

On Bourbon, smoke wafts through the air
like bitter cotton candy,
while lucid noise procures the scent
of whisky, wine, and brandy.

My wild path connects with strangers
as they, as well, pass through,
and yet, I'm shy (a foreign guy!)
I sit and sigh, and read Camus.

And though beyond that double door
the city streets are hostile,
my thoughts are safe to roam and write
in this warm and tidy hostel.
