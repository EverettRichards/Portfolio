3/13/23

I've hitherto wondered,
How much I've refined.
Today I am polished,
like shoes freshly shined.

But yesterday notice,
acknowledge the noise,
to uncouth behavior, say,
"Boys will be boys."

At birth I look upward
I take in what's shown
But in times of conflict
I feel all alone

I measure my angle
I calibrate gently
But scope is confined
By life's silver century

Ignore every semblance
of sanity's reason,
for hours mature
as forests change season.

What early brought verdant
at present is browning,
tomorrow dismembered,
the trees all a-frowning.

Throughout my short years
I have wandered and wondered
I've heeded the weather
As shrouded skies thundered.

But when grave is near
After years of rehearsin'
I cannot ignore
That I'm still the same person.
