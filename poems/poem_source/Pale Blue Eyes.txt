WIP

Feckless men chase stormy skies
that sail the breeze to Brooklyn;
so reckless, then, are pale-blue eyes
that my own gazers look in.

In her hand, a household brand,
o' timid Ticond'roga;
a yellow shaft of octal craft,
her page bright white like a toga,

that guards and keeps her busy mind,
so full of thoughts and focus,
while unaware, I admire her hair,
her heart my own heart's locus.

This night would not have been so right
if I had lacked the courage
to let down-fall my castle wall,
my moat, my throne, my burrage.

Yet here I type of bounties ripe,
but see no gleam of ore,
and count before the cards are dealt
my sorrow-stricken score.
