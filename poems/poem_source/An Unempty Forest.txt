3/26/22

A man walks alone on a moonlit forest trail
Searching for shelter, alas to no avail
Tomorrow he will find it, for he can never fail
But tonight, he knows, he must bear the wind and hail

The forest's gentle melodies creep throughout the air
Of a hidden watcher, the man is unaware
A sense of unknown company warns him to prepare
For it is still uncertain how deftly he will fare

The watcher breaks the shadows of an oak tree most sublime
In silence so supreme that one could sense a fallen dime
I cannot think of many ways to carry on the rhyme
For he, like me, does find himself so very short on time
