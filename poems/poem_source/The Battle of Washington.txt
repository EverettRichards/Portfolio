3/21/22

Two armies stand face-to-face along an undeclared aisle of contention.
Both armies send an officer to the aisle to negotiate the terms of the other side's surrender.
The officers meet and shake hands, holding back expressions of distaste and distrust.
The first officer claims he seeks peace, fairness, and liberty.
The second officer advocates for order, justice, and freedom.
They bicker and debate as the sun and moon battle overhead for control of the sky,
but neither officer is willing to concede as the celestials nobly do.
The two armies occasionally call reinforcements,
and delegate leave as the battlefield's capacity dictates,
but neither gains any ground,
for the belligerents of the Battle of Washington would rather do nothing
than fight side-by-side for their true common cause.
