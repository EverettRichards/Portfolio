3/21/22

An endless ocean stretches wearily to a static horizon.
A towering bluff overlooks the war-torn seastrand.
Miles of barbed wire guard the shores from a long forgotten enemy.
Under six inches of earth, industrial fingerprints await an unlucky wanderer.

The call of uncivilized progress echoes on the hills.
Children scramble to return to their fathers and mothers.
Families trade a life on the surface for one in the underworld.
Under six feet of earth, the people funnel into the shelters they never hoped to occupy.

As the sun collides with the sea, another rises in its place.
A mighty tree quickly sprouts, fifteen miles off the Pacific shoreline.
The twilight sky is shrouded by a hazy silver ploom.
Under six feet of earth, all is silent.
