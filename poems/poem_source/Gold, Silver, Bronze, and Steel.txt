3/21/23

It's harder to practice than to learn,
to teach than to study,
to give than to take,
to fall than to recuperate
energies spent
and steps untaken

To know was once to wonder,
to find to sometime seek,
to burden once to value,
to grow to once be meek

Ask not about the soul
whose finance so endeavors
to corners better left unswept,
and to uncertain truths

Afraid to learn the way,
afraid of being lost,
to spend one's lifetime worrying
is life's unwanted cost

There is no gold tomorrow
No silver lines the present
No bronze adorns the past
Whose echoes ripple out

Steel alone persists
From time to lonely time
For concrete crumbles, buildings shatter,
but only steel can rhyme

To ripple in the waves,
to bend when threatened deep,
to be indifferent under stress,
to welcome moonlight's sleep,
Oblivious to those that creep.
