5/8/26

She said goodbye this morning.
Or maybe it was last night.
Many moments have since passed me by,
leaving me hollow and delicate,
like those cheap chocolate rabbits
my grandma used to send me
to commemorate the undead savior.

She said she was sorry.
Or maybe she was just lonely,
trying to shed her throbbing pain
as I dug a deep hole
for my own cold-shocked heart,
like that split-second panic
that rattles your veins
when you plunge into a frigid pool
after soaking for too long
in a sultry, soothing hot tub.

She said she had misunderstood.
Or maybe she had been misled,
her mind twisted by malice
protruded by a false saint,
like a greedy fortune-teller
who foretells your bounty
while scraping her own
from your thinning pocket.

I look ahead fondly,
but wearily, too,
and I know that someday,
all good things must end;
and yet I'll enjoy
the time I have left
and the beautiful moments
we'll continue to share.
