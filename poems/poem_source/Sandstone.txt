2/21/23

The sandstone preserves,
The memorable tales,
Of storms and of fires,
Of foresty trails.

The bluffs by the ocean,
They tower so high,
But onlookers caution,
As they wander by.

The stones serve a purpose,
To tell stories told,
To write down our history,
The new and the old.

They tell of the future,
Of tumbling boulders,
Of life's unsung burdens,
Too heavy to shoulder.

Together lie past, present, future, alike,
From slate-battered tombstones, to baby's first bike,
Contained in the sandstone that watches the world,
An eon of sorrow, unraveled, unfurled.

My past is for certain,
My wrongs and my rights,
But future's dark curtain,
Shrouds uncertain nights.

I know where I've been,
Time's river is flowing,
So beseech I the sandstone,
"Where am I going?"
