3/17/22

A river called "anywhere" traverses an unknown prairie
Confined by its size and its will
Through valleys and hills it wanders
Longing for the mighty Ocean

I sit upon the fallen trunk of a fearsome Oak
I surrender to the current of the narrow brook
Adrift in search of a foreign sea
Longing for emancipation

A seagull flashes its brilliant wings overhead
Hope strikes the deepest corridors of my heart
The stream follows my strides onward
The graceful bird rides on a stream of salty perfume

I feel the stream widen and fall still
The delta reveals an infinite sea of diamonds and pearls
The undertow carries me wherever I command
I look up at the seagull and extend to it a smile of gratitude

I am free at last.
