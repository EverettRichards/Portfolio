11/23/23

*I wrote this poem while listening to "The Boxer" by Carbon Leaf and then the very different homonymous
song recorded by Mumford & Sons, a cover of the original by Simon & Garfunkel.

Two by two, the soaring aspens
Fell to the dirt, sore and spent
Many were near, yet no sound was made
A call unheeded by deaf ears

In the foliage, spiders danced
And spun their webs with thorough care
But their silky homes were torn and stripped
By beings supreme and indifferent

Ragged in their homeworld
Now barren and forsaken
The critters of the forest
Seek refuge in what was taken

Never again to see beyond
The snow-kissed mountaintops
Their peaks breaking softly
Against a sky of fluffy white

A crisis in disguise
