5/6/26

The breeze casts itself into my lungs
as if to remind me to breathe,
and the sunlight shines
off the blue-glass dome
in a strange way,
like it never had before
-- or, perhaps it had,
and I just never noticed --
and for the last time, I stand here,
hovering over this sea-green guardrail
while anxious ants crawl and meander
along palm-speckled Centennial Walkway,
entrapped by their thoughts,
oblivious to mine,
and briefly,
I imagine that I know
what my grandfather saw
when stricken with the star-sickness,
as he crudely inhaled
his last, shallow breath
before crossing the threshold
of the knowing and the known;
and then, in a whir,
my focus returns
to the golden-hour light-shower
that decks the shallow hills
and traces the wobbly horizon,
and I know that I will ne'er again
be graced by the splendor
of this fine vantage point,
but will trade it for summits
I haven't yet seen,
overlooking mirrored oceans
where the sea-strand pummels the waves
and where that life-giving yolk
is born, and not swallowed
by monstrous blue depths;
and I know
-- though I'm hesitant to admit it --
that this new world I stumble to
is not as splendid as I had once thought,
and yet it'll be worth
every mile I conquer
from that soaring steel bird,
every memory that fades
as new ones arise,
and every breath that my lungs withdraw
from the Earth's love-torn blanket.
