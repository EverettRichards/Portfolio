8/12/22

The streets are decked with starstruck folks,
Seeking escape from the air that chokes,
Wondering where they can tie their yokes,
They never have been to Alaska.

Some dream in the night, some say it feels right,
Some wander around, just barely upright,
Some get their desire from heavenly light,
They all want to go to Alaska.

Some go by sky, some go by water,
Some say it's cold, some say it gets hotter,
Some drive all the way, with son and with daughter,
They make their way to Alaska.

A poet does cry, at stories untold,
A gambler raises, while other ones fold,
A mother comforts the child that she holds,
They search for their homes in Alaska.

A river runs fast, a glacier runs slow,
the oceans run high, the Northern Lights glow,
A body is trembling, from bare head to toe,
They've all found their homes in Alaska.
