2/5/22

Marble and lumber comprise mighty halls
People do slumber behind stucco walls

Dark ears fall deaf to the tune of a bird
Perverse in the manner of words overheard

To run and to yell is to set free desire
To hasten to fall, to fall to the mire

Above the rustled and chirping birds fly
An unheard attempt to pierce through the sky

A blanket, a layer, to keep safe our home
At night, a shroud to protect those who roam

Aloof from the river, whose melody cries
For faraway places where tall mountains rise

And fall to the valleys, so green and so vast
Where trickling rivers descend oh-so fast

The sun paints the wheat hues of sulfur and cream
The moonlight reflects off a forgotten stream

As dusk briskly frisks the warm-water weeds
Nature inquires what man only needs

To live and to thrive, to find what's been hidden
To saddle up horses not meant to be ridden

Awake are the pieces of nature's great puzzle
Didactic and yearning to break off its muzzle

Unsilenced, the woods host a chorus of voices
With no aching worry for duties and choices

But the wind shares its secrets with no earthly child,
For only a human hears the call of the wild.
