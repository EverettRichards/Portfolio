5/29/26

I sit here, soundly, on a coarse fabric seat
and attend to a medley of pop music and my own thoughts,
while sunny summer travelers make their hurried ways
towards cities and skies I haven't yet tried.

Meanwhile, a neon banner boasts
that "life is better buzzed";
then I think to the seltzer I drank 'fore the bus,
and my pink cheeks hum in solemn agreement.

Then I indulged, I'll admit, in a fine salty treat
of 'taters and flour and sweet, tender meat,
that burdened my wallet a whole three dollars more
than cost me the privilege of passage to 'Frisco.

And sure, I know well, as for eight hours I dwell,
that the same would adorn a salty coast-hugging drive,
but my ego is lifted by knowing I've won
my way to frontiers far above the I-5.

I still don't quite know where I'm going, or why,
but I feel discontent with the burrow I'm leaving,
and I feel that something awaits for me in the sky;
something I could never reach from the ground.

So I ask you, dearest conscience,
as my eyes trace those tri-colored angels,
sliding through their heavenly gates,
what novelty do the stars afford me?
