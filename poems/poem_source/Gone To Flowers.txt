10/16/22

How many hours jam between noon and one
Time slowly chases, but you needn't run

From one to two, time passes through
Some passed away, while others grew

By three, the hours come and go
For future hours dare to row

At four, the minutes disappear
While hours only persevere

At five, you know the night is near
As twilight birds sing in your ear

By six, you know it's half-way done
Oh, the good times, back at one!

By seven, dusk still lingers by
You've tried to stop it, I know, you try.

But eight means four are left to pass
By now, it's getting pretty fast

Nine is here, the sky is dark
The bells chime on. Listen! Hark!

Ten, you must slow down.

Eleven, turn around!

Twelve.
