* This poem is about the impending crisis presented by artificial intelligence (the Zephyr), spearheaded by techno-industrialists who promise economic upheaval without a coherent plan for the alternative.

A zealous green Zephyr caresses the Earth,
leaving mildewy musk in its woeful wake.

Meek men hunch on shoulders of disparate giants,
their brawny hands bruised from heavyweight brawls,
while surface-born darlings sit dangling from doorways
and trod head-slung in their tenement halls,
their feet cold and bare amidst winter's defiance.

The wind carries promise to beckon an era
of lazy-cat sun-naps and pompous peruse,
yet offers no solace for withering purpose
but the tender indulgence of whiskey-brown booze.

And when the Pentecostal nightmares have abated,
what will remain but the smithering delinquency of yesteryear?
