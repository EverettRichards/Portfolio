2/21/23

"Hello," a call that splinters walls
Cracks open roofs
And shatters the halls

Atop silver linen lies cottonwood flakes
Peeled and discarded
A crumbled charcoal core

Why do these words so pierce my heart?
Like a manta ray's spear
Like Neptune's turquoise trident

Magenta streaks shroud a papyrus sheet
Silent in their contempt
For man's written word

"Goodbye," a final message, a concluding act
Already fate has been set
Its maker, unmade, forever.
