11/23/23

That day was so perfect, that night just the same
We wandered in circles with no one to blame

Just happy to walk in each other's warm glow
Above the cascades that thundered below

The venue was lovely, the food even better
The weather was warm — no need for a sweater

We journeyed back home to walk even more
Our hearts kept the beat, but neither kept score

Of the hours that passed like swift cars on a turnpike
Or like gophers and squirrels on a mountain-trail hike

We touched for a moment, the world stood still
Not one blade of grass swayed on any green hill

I gave her my hand, and she gave me her own
Together we sat, no longer alone

She kissed my cold cheek with lips soft and sweet
I gave my reply, and our souls did meet

She asked me if she and I were now real
And I briskly felt things I never thought I'd feel

I said yes, of course, and we shared the cold night
Prowling in the darkness, with no need for light

Our path led us past the all-grand Hepner Hall
Its vibrant white walls ascending so tall

And there before us, just past 10:07
Was a sign that foretold of a "Match Made in Heaven"

And now I still wonder, as I sit here and write
Where I would be now if not for that night

A "Match Made in Heaven" was certainly made
By forces beyond any cards I have played

By sheer divine will, my life is now paired
And no longer do I feel alone and scared

But content and at peace with my life as it is
Prepared for a future of harmony and bliss

With a girl, once a stranger, nothing less than my own
Now my friend and dear lover, my companion and home
