2/6/23

Abrasive but tender, her skin against mine
My fingers search for her love, so near but ever-evasive
My heartbeat quickens as she takes away my breath
Gone are the days when I asked not how, but when
Today my lips sing as I ambush her bronze body
A strand of charcoal hair falls astray upon her eyelids
Beneath, her starstruck pupils are blind to strife and entropy
My consciousness yearning, I bide my time until Tomorrow's sun unzips an eager sky

The dawn, the dusk, the silent thrill of young love
All present inside me
Never entertaining the wherefore and why.
Answers will find your burning questions
As the burning sun finds a moonlit forest
Solitary in its unborn embers
Distant calls of morning echo
The new day greets a billion faces

Tomorrow
